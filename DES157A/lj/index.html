May 17th, 2021

Because of the topic of our studio this time, the article Game Design UX Best Practices by Amir Dori is pretty effective and provides many useful ideas and details about game design for me. Although this article is mainly focus on the mobile phone games, some of the techniques are useful in the web game design. Several parts that I found interesting are sliders, pop-ups, and user choices. There are many design tips related to in-game purchases and ratings in the article, but they are not something I would use in web design at this time.

One simple but effective technique is that using different colors to create different expression for users. For example, the option that good for the game can be green and be placed on the right, while the option that bad for the game can be red and on the right. Also, when the users click on the dim background, the pop-ups should be closed as a cancel button is clicked.

According to my own observation, games designed for vertical screen is much more user-friendly than horizontal, because they only need one hand to play, and the comfort-zone to reach is larger.

April 28th, 2021

In Michael Gonchar’s article, he shared 10 intriguing photographs that he thought can improve viewers’ close reading and visual thinking skills. Individual photographs may be able to represent a complete story, but how to arouse the reader's curiosity to explore the details hidden in the images is a question that needs further consideration.

What is difference between photographs and websites is that users can interact with the website itself, and the action that clicking in a website already represent the curiosity of the user to gain the information inside the site. So, the easiest and most intuitive way is to make the site cooler and to present more information to the reader with maximum efficiency in the process of this interactivity.

As a case study for this studio, I browsed LENS by Science and was surprised to realize how interactive a professional web designer can make a website. The pages themselves are dynamic throughout the browsing process, and the switching between pages is animated. I think the most interesting point is the change of the mouse cursor: when the user's cursor crosses a specific text or image, the area represented by the cursor will be enlarged and more detailed information will be provided in this area. In addition, the design of the site has a lot of white space and leaves the main visual area for images rather than text.

April 22th, 2021

The article Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows introduced several interesting details about the overlaying windows displayed on the website.

In my practical use, the modals are usually the login or registration screens. This article mentions that error, success, and warning messages should not be displayed as overlay windows, because they are likely to forget the details on them after the user closes the interface. But I think these types of windows can also be used as overlay windows to deepen the user's impression, as long as the information they express will stay on the main page.

I think we should make multiple sketches before confirming the style and size of the pop-up window, and think about what style is the most convenient and appropriate in terms of user experience.

April 15th, 2021

The article Best Practices for Form Design provides many useful techniques that helps to increase the user experience when inserting a form inside a website. There are many details mentioned in the article that I had never noticed before, such as how to distinguish optional and required fields. In my actual use, there are actually many websites that still use the wrong examples mentioned in the article, such as using asterisk to mark optional items.

One of the things that surprised me the most was the statement in the project about using placeholder text that the disadvantages of placeholder texts outweigh the advantages in practice, so it is better to put it outside the text box as a hint. Based on my personal user experience alone, placeholder text is very convenient. This comparison makes me realize that the vast majority of users' experiences may be different from my own. In the design process, it is important to consider solving most of the problems that may exist.

I think the form design of google account sign up page is a good example. Although it has placeholder text, when user click the textbox, the placeholder text would disappear, and the same text appears above the textbox instead, to remind the user what should they input.
